Who am I?

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Ubiquitous Interaction Research Group, Department of Computer Science, University of Helsinki, where I received my Ph.D. I received a Master of Science in System Information Sciences from Tohoku University, Japan, and a Bachelor of Engineering in Digital Media Technology from Zhejiang University, China.

My research interests include information visualization and human-centered AI.

Here are several projects I did.

The CO2 Explorer identifies interaction targets, including charts, chart components, and prior notes, as entities and enables users to cite relevant entities in notes to support their insight narratives. Prototype Source code

The improved version of MediSyn synthesizes five drug-target datasets. We also redesigned the interaction of MediSyn based on the notion of entities (that is, taking drugs and targets as entities) for insight generation. MediSyn supports five types of interactions: 1) selecting entities of interest, 2) connecting relevant entities, 3) elaborating by retrieving the details of entity relations, 4) exploring other entities, and 5) entity-based insight sharing.

MediSyn: Uncertainty-aware visualization of multiple biomedical datasets to support drug treatment selection

The first version of MediSyn synthesizes two drug-target datasets. It uses a matrix-based layout to visually link drugs, targets (i.e., mutations), and tumor types. Data uncertainties are salient in MediSyn as (i) missing data are exposed through the matrix view; (ii) inconsistencies between datasets are shown via overlaid layers; and (iii) data credibility is conveyed through links to data provenance.

Tuple retrieval visualizes search results under various combinations of search terms. It also suggests relevant terms and combinations of terms to help navigate the search space.

Scalable exploration of relevance prospects to support decision making

IntersectionExplorer applied the design of UpSet to inter-relate publications generated by recommendation agents and other users (such as bookmarks) with the idea that the item suggested by multiple sources can increase the likelihood of user adoption of it. Source code

There are a series of extensions I made for the dynamic and flexible interactive photoshow (D-Flip) as my master's research project. The extension includes adapting the system to various types of content, such as paintings and flashcards, and various interaction modalities, such as multi-touch public displays and whole-body interactions. Teaching

I teach the Interaction Data Visualization course at the University of Helsinki as part of the Master's Program in Data science and Computer Science. Check out the lecture slides below.

An introduction of data visualization

As an introduction to data visualization, this lecture discusses what is data visualization, why do we need visualization, and what are the purposes of visualization. It also introduces several common techniques of visualizing multi-dimensional, graph, and set-typed data.

Human perception

Topics include optical illusions, visual processing model, preattentive processing, Gestalt laws, etc.

Design principles

This lecture talks about the LATCH principle for data organization and Tufte design principles.

Visualization design

This lecture talks about the nested model for visualization design proposed by Tamara Munzner and two case studies.

Multi-dimensional data visualization

This lecture talks about techniques of visualizing one, two, and multi-dimensional data, as well as the use of composite views for multi-dimensional data representation.

Graph & tree visualization

This lecture talks about techniques to visualize graphs (e.g., node-link diagrams and adjacency matrix) and trees (e.g., space-filling techniques).

Set-typed data visualization

This lecture is about techniques to visualize set-typed data, such as Venn and Euler diagrams.

Interacting with visualizations

This lecture talks about seven categories of interactions in visualization (Yi et al., 2007), as well as methods of interacting with focused and contextual views (Cockburn et al., 2009).

Visualization evaluation

This lecture discusses quantitative and qualitative evaluation methods in visualization.

Human-centered AI

This lecture talks about the concepts of human-centered AI and the role of visualization involved.
